# Smooth Fence USA — Permits & HOA Reference

Detail on HOA and permit process. Companion to SKILL.md.

## HOA overview

Many neighborhoods across Flagler, Volusia, St. Johns, Duval, and Putnam
counties are part of a Homeowners Association (HOA). HOAs commonly
regulate:

- Maximum and minimum fence height
- Allowed materials (vinyl/aluminum/wood/chain-link)
- Allowed styles (privacy, semi-privacy, picket, etc.)
- Allowed colors
- Setback from property line
- Front-yard fence rules (often more restrictive than backyard)
- Gate style and placement

Failure to follow HOA rules can result in: rejected application, fines
after install, removal order.

## How SFUSA helps with HOA

1. **Pre-quote review.** Before signing a contract, SFUSA reviews the
   homeowner's HOA design guidelines to confirm the proposed
   style/material/color is allowed.
2. **Drawings & descriptions.** SFUSA provides drawings, dimensions, and
   product descriptions to support the homeowner's application.
3. **Style/color suggestions.** If the homeowner's initial preference is
   not HOA-compliant, SFUSA suggests near-equivalent options that are.
4. **Application support.** The homeowner submits the application
   (SFUSA does not submit on behalf of the homeowner in most HOAs).

## Permit overview

Some — not all — fence projects in Northeast Florida require a city or
county permit. Whether a permit is required depends on:

- Jurisdiction (city limits vs unincorporated county)
- Fence height (over a certain threshold typically requires a permit)
- Material (some materials trigger thresholds)
- Location (front yard, side yard, easement)
- Lot type (waterfront, corner, conservation)

## How SFUSA helps with permits

1. **Identify requirement.** SFUSA tells the homeowner if a permit is
   needed based on the project specifics.
2. **Coordinate steps.** When applicable, SFUSA coordinates permit
   submission, inspection scheduling, and final sign-off.
3. **Layout compliance.** SFUSA designs fence layouts that respect
   property lines and easements.

## Property lines & easements

SFUSA plans layouts considering property lines and easements as
provided by the homeowner (survey, plat, or deed).

**Important:** SFUSA does not perform property line surveys. Final
boundary determinations come from a licensed surveyor or the proper
authority. If the homeowner is uncertain, SFUSA recommends a survey
before installation begins to avoid disputes with neighbors.

## Pool gate compliance (Florida)

Florida pool code typically requires:

- Self-closing gate hardware
- Self-latching gate hardware
- Latch positioned at least 54 inches above grade
- Gate swings outward (away from the pool)

SFUSA installs pool-code-compliant hardware on request when a pool gate
is in scope.
